Earth

Type: Terrestrial planet Distance from Sun: 149.6 million km (1 AU) Diameter: 12,742 km Orbital Period: 365.25 Earth days Key Facts: Earth is the only known planet with liquid water on its surface and an atmosphere conducive to life as we know it.

Mars

Type: Terrestrial planet Distance from Sun: 227.9 million km (1.52 AU) Diameter: 6,779 km Orbital Period: 687 Earth days Key Facts: Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and is the target for current and future missions looking for signs of past life.

Jupiter

Type: Gas giant Distance from Sun: 778.6 million km (5.20 AU) Diameter: 139,820 km Orbital Period: 11.86 Earth years Key Facts: Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System, with a massive storm called the Great Red Spot, which has been raging for centuries.

Saturn

Type: Gas giant Distance from Sun: 1.43 billion km (9.58 AU) Diameter: 116,460 km Orbital Period: 29.46 Earth years Key Facts: Saturn is known for its extensive ring system, made of ice and rock particles.

Type: Ice giant Distance from Sun: 2.87 billion km (19.18 AU) Diameter: 50,724 km Orbital Period: 84 Earth years Key Facts: Uranus is tilted on its side, making its axial tilt the most extreme of any planet in the Solar System.

Type: Ice giant Distance from Sun: 4.50 billion km (30.07 AU) Diameter: 49,244 km Orbital Period: 164.8 Earth years Key Facts: Neptune has the strongest winds in the Solar System, and its atmosphere contains large storms, including the Great Dark Spot.
